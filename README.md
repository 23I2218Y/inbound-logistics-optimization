## 🧩 Situation

A global apparel and footwear company operates a complex international supply chain, sourcing products from factories across Asia and distributing through multiple U.S. distribution centers. The inbound transportation network was experiencing critical inefficiencies — delays in shipments, unpredictable delivery schedules, and misaligned resource allocation — creating downstream disruptions across distribution operations.

---

## 🎯 Objective

Analyze and optimize the company's inbound transportation network by:
- Identifying root causes of transit delays and delivery schedule variability
- Evaluating port-specific performance across major U.S. entry ports
- Comparing year-over-year operational trends
- Providing data-driven, actionable recommendations to improve efficiency

**KPIs Tracked:**
| KPI | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Factory-to-Port Transit Time | Min / Median / Max by origin & port |
| Port-to-Distribution Center Transit Time | Baseline vs. outlier analysis |
| Shipment Volume Distribution | % share by port |
| Transit Variability | Standard spread & outlier identification |

---

## ⚙️ Actions Taken

### 1. Data Collection & Cleaning
- Analyzed real shipment datasets provided by the client (multi-year period)
- Excluded pandemic-era data to avoid statistical distortions
- Applied the **80/20 Pareto principle** — focused analysis on ports handling the majority of shipment volume

### 2. Port-Specific Performance Analysis
Evaluated major U.S. destination ports across Gulf Coast and West Coast:

| Port Type | Volume Tier | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Primary Gulf Coast port | High volume | Largest hub; notable outliers on select Asian origin routes |
| Primary West Coast port | High volume | High throughput but highest transit time variability |
| Secondary West Coast port | Mid volume | Most consistent performance; low variability |
| East Coast port | Low volume | Underutilized; strategic opportunity for network load balancing |

### 3. Transit Time Distribution Analysis
- **Factory-to-Port:** Most shipments clustered within a standard transit window; a defined peak range identified as the operational benchmark
- **Port-to-Distribution Center:** Majority of shipments completed within target timeframe; sharp drop-off for extended durations
- Identified critical outliers with significantly prolonged transit times requiring root cause investigation

### 4. Geographical Route Mapping
- Mapped factory-to-port shipping routes across major Asian manufacturing origins
- Analyzed routing strategy differences between Gulf Coast ports (via Panama Canal) and West Coast ports (direct trans-Pacific routes)
- Identified route-specific congestion patterns and origin-destination misalignments

### 5. Year-over-Year Comparison
- Gulf Coast port: Measurable improvement in transit times and efficiency over the comparison period
- Primary West Coast port: Continued delays and high variability — identified as priority for intervention
- Secondary West Coast port: Sustained consistent performance with minor optimization opportunities

### 6. Root Cause Analysis
Identified 4 core root causes of inefficiency:
1. **Port Congestion** — Primary ports overloaded, creating bottlenecks
2. **Route Dependency** — Excessive concentration of volume at a single port and specific origin routes
3. **Operational Inefficiencies** — Underutilization of alternative ports; no active load balancing
4. **Inadequate Monitoring** — Lack of real-time tracking to catch and address anomalies proactively

---

## 📊 Key Findings & Results

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| 📍 Gulf Coast port bottlenecks | Select origin routes showed significantly extended maximum transit times |
| 📍 West Coast variability | Certain origin routes showed extreme spread between median and maximum transit times |
| 📍 Secondary West Coast strength | Most reliable port — identified as best candidate for volume increase |
| 📍 East Coast port untapped | Minimal utilization despite strong potential to relieve pressure on primary ports |
| 📍 Year-over-year improvement | Efficiency gains confirmed across Gulf Coast and secondary West Coast ports |
| 📍 Distribution center efficiency | Strong baseline performance for most shipments; anomalies isolated and flagged |

---

## 💡 Recommendations Delivered

1. **Investigate primary port outliers** — root cause analysis on highest-variability origin routes
2. **Redistribute shipments to secondary and alternative ports** — balance network load, reduce congestion
3. **Implement real-time tracking & predictive analytics** — early detection of delay patterns
4. **Optimize scheduling at high-variability ports** — better demand forecasting and labor planning
5. **Diversify origin-destination pairings** — reduce dependency on overloaded route combinations
